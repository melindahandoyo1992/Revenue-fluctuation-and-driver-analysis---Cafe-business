# Revenue-fluctuation-and-driver-analysis---Cafe-business

## Executive Summary

Revenue performance is primarily driven by product mix and order volume.
	•	The revenue peak in June was driven by a higher proportion of mid- to high-priced products
	•	The revenue decline in July was driven by a decrease in order volume, especially in low- to mid-priced items

This shows that revenue is not solely dependent on transaction volume, but also on the type of products sold

⸻

## Business Problem

Identify the key drivers behind revenue fluctuations:
	•	Order volume
	•	Product mix
	•	Pricing

⸻

## Dataset Overview

Transactional café sales data including:
	-	Transaction date
	- 	Item purchased
	-	Price per unit
	-	Quantity

⸻

Analytical Approach

A driver-based framework was used:
	-	Order Volume → number of transactions
	-	Product Mix → composition of items sold (price & category)
	

Analysis included:
	-	Monthly comparison (Feb, Mar, Jun, Jul)
	-	Volume trend analysis
	-	Price distribution analysis
	-	Item-level revenue contribution

⸻

Key Insights

📈 Revenue Increase (June)
	•	June generated the highest revenue despite lower order volume than March
	•	March had higher volume, but was dominated by lower-priced products
	•	June sales shifted toward mid- to high-priced items (e.g., sandwiches, smoothies)

Insight: Revenue growth was driven by product mix, not volume

⸻

📉 Revenue Decrease (July)
	•	Revenue decline was driven by a drop in order volume and quantity
	•	Low- to mid-priced items (e.g., beverages, ~$3 range) showed the largest decrease

Insight: Revenue decline was driven by reduced demand in lower-priced products

⸻

Impact
	•	Revenue is influenced by both customer volume and product mix
	•	High-priced items can increase revenue without increasing traffic
	•	Decline in lower-priced product demand can significantly impact total revenue

⸻

Recommendations
	•	Strengthen high-value product strategy
Promote mid- to high-priced items (e.g., bundles, cashback incentives)
	•	Optimize product mix through bundling
Combine low-performing items with high-performing products to increase basket value
	•	Stabilize demand during low periods
Use targeted promotions to recover order volume
